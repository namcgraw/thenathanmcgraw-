Mortgage Lending Glossary

Comprehensive mortgage terminology for Chico California homebuyers:

Pre-Approval
A lender's written commitment stating how much money a borrower can borrow based on verification of income, assets, credit, and employment. Nathan McGraw provides thorough pre-approvals for Chico homebuyers.
Pre-Qualification
An informal estimate of borrowing capacity based on unverified information provided by the borrower. Less reliable than pre-approval for Butte County home purchases.
Down Payment
The initial payment made when purchasing a home, typically expressed as a percentage of the purchase price. Requirements vary by loan type: FHA requires 3.5%, conventional requires 3-20%, VA and USDA require 0%.
Closing Costs
Fees and expenses paid at closing when finalizing a home purchase, typically ranging from 2-5% of the purchase price in Butte County.
Interest Rate
The percentage charged by a lender for borrowing money, significantly affecting monthly payment amounts and total interest paid over the loan term.
APR - Annual Percentage Rate
The total cost of borrowing including interest rate and fees, expressed as a yearly percentage. Provides better comparison between loan offers than interest rate alone.
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)
Insurance required on conventional loans with less than 20% down payment, protecting the lender if the borrower defaults.
Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)
Insurance required on FHA loans, consisting of upfront premium (1.75% of loan amount) and annual premiums paid monthly throughout the loan term.
Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)
The percentage of gross monthly income used to pay monthly debt obligations. Most loan programs require DTI ratios below 43-50%.
Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)
The ratio of the loan amount to the property's appraised value or purchase price, whichever is less.
Escrow
A neutral third party that handles funds and documents during a real estate transaction, ensuring all conditions are met before closing.
Escrow Account
An account maintained by the lender to hold funds for property taxes and insurance, paid monthly as part of the mortgage payment.
Underwriting
The process of evaluating a loan application to determine risk and eligibility based on credit, income, assets, and property value.
Appraisal
A professional assessment of a property's market value conducted by a licensed appraiser, required by lenders before approving mortgages.
Title Insurance
Insurance protecting buyers and lenders against defects in property title, such as liens, encumbrances, or ownership disputes.
Earnest Money
A deposit made by homebuyers to demonstrate serious intent to purchase, typically 1-3% of purchase price, held in escrow until closing.
Contingency
Conditions that must be met for a real estate contract to be binding, such as financing approval, satisfactory inspection, or appraisal meeting value.
Rate Lock
An agreement between borrower and lender guaranteeing a specific interest rate for a set period, protecting against rate increases during processing.
Points
Fees paid to lenders at closing to reduce interest rates, with one point equaling 1% of the loan amount.
Origination Fee
Fee charged by lenders for processing mortgage applications, typically 0.5-1% of loan amount.
Amortization
The process of paying off a loan through regular payments over time, with each payment covering interest and principal.
Principal
The original loan amount borrowed, or the remaining balance owed on a mortgage excluding interest.
Fixed-Rate Mortgage
A mortgage with an interest rate that remains constant throughout the loan term, providing payment stability.
Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM)
A mortgage with an interest rate that changes periodically based on market indices, typically starting with lower rates than fixed mortgages.
Conventional Loan
A mortgage not insured or guaranteed by the federal government, typically requiring higher credit scores and down payments than government-backed loans.
FHA Loan
A mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration, offering low down payments and flexible credit requirements for qualified borrowers.
VA Loan
A mortgage guaranteed by the Department of Veterans Affairs for eligible veterans, active-duty military, and surviving spouses, offering zero down payment.
USDA Loan
A mortgage guaranteed by the United States Department of Agriculture for eligible rural properties, offering zero down payment for qualified borrowers.
Jumbo Loan
A mortgage exceeding conforming loan limits set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, requiring stricter qualification standards.
Conforming Loan
A mortgage meeting Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac guidelines for loan amount, credit, and documentation requirements.
Cash-Out Refinance
Refinancing for more than the current loan balance, allowing borrowers to receive the difference in cash for other purposes.
Streamline Refinance
Simplified refinancing options for FHA and VA loans with reduced documentation and sometimes no appraisal required.
Home Equity
The difference between a property's current market value and the outstanding mortgage balance, representing the owner's actual ownership stake.
Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
Documentation from the Department of Veterans Affairs verifying a veteran's eligibility for VA loan benefits.
Good Faith Estimate (GFE)
Historical term for estimated loan costs; replaced by Loan Estimate form showing projected costs and terms.
Loan Estimate
Standardized form provided within three business days of application, detailing estimated loan terms, costs, and features.
Closing Disclosure
Final statement of loan terms and closing costs provided at least three business days before closing, replacing the HUD-1 settlement statement.
TRID
TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure rule requiring specific timing and format for loan disclosures to protect consumers.
Credit Score
Numerical representation of creditworthiness based on credit history, ranging from 300-850, significantly affecting loan approval and interest rates.
FICO Score
Credit score calculated by Fair Isaac Corporation, most commonly used by mortgage lenders for loan decisions.
Credit Report
Detailed record of an individual's credit history, including payment history, outstanding debts, and public records.
Automated Underwriting System (AUS)
Computer-based system that evaluates loan applications against lender guidelines, providing approval recommendations.
Loan Commitment
Written promise from a lender to make a loan under specified terms and conditions.
Clear to Close
Status indicating all underwriting conditions are satisfied and the loan is approved for closing.
Funding
The final step when the lender releases loan proceeds to complete the purchase transaction.
Recording
The process of filing the deed and mortgage with county records, making the transaction a matter of public record.
Seasoning
Requirement that borrowers wait a minimum period after certain credit events before qualifying for new mortgages.
Reserves
Liquid assets remaining after closing, typically measured in months of mortgage payments, required by lenders for loan approval.
Gift Funds
Money given to borrowers by family members or other acceptable sources for down payment or closing costs, requiring proper documentation.
Seller Concessions
Contributions from sellers toward buyers' closing costs, with limits based on loan type and down payment amount.
